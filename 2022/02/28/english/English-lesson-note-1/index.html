# Feb 28 2022 Lesson Note

# Article : We are as smart as rocket scientists

Most of us have the impression that rocket scientists and brain
surgeons are super-brainy. Scientists have conducted research
that suggests members of the general public are actually just as smart as those high- flyers. Researchers from University College London asked 329 aerospace engineers and 72 neurosurgeons to complete tests designed to assess six different areas of cognition. The researchers compared their data with those from 18,257 respondents who had previously taken the tests. The researchers said: "Only two differences were significant: the neurosurgeons' problem-solving speed was quicker and their memory recall speed was slower." (這裡是 compare to ordinary people)

The research questions the validity of using English phrases such as "this isn't rocket science" or "that isn't brain surgery". We use these expressions to suggest something is actually quite easy to understand. Are aeronautics and neuroscience so mindboggling? The researchers shed some light on this. They said: "In situations that do not require rapid problem solving, it might be more correct to use 'It's not brain surgery', but in situations where rapid information recall is needed this phrase should be avoided." This gives hope to all of us that we can embark on careers we once disregarded because of "genius-level" requirements. However, this could lead to a glut of brain surgeons and rocket designers.

# Vocab

Word / phrasemeaning
1. super brainyreally smart; 這個詞也很常用
2. mindbogglingpuzzling/overwhelming；不等於 unbelivable；比如說很多人投票給 trump 是件很 mindboggling 的事
3. It's not rocket science當要某人花很長時間去解決一件超級容易的事時，可以用這句； "Have you solved that math problem? It's not rocket science you know."
4. It's not brain surgeryused to say that you do not think that something is very difficult to do or to understand: 跟上面的相似。
5. shed some light on this把某事情弄很更清楚更容易懂
6. torch to shine light on sth同上
7. glufexcess
8. steered clear ofavoided; 通常可以用於 physical 的東西，但 mentally 也可以；比如 steered clear of any political argument.

# Shed some light on these words...

# Conduct & leading

中文翻譯 conduct (carried out) 和 lead 都是差不多，有 "帶領，指揮" 的意思，一直都沒分清楚... 今天問了 Conduct a project/carry out a project 是對的嗎？然後 Lisa 說覺得很奇怪，她說 lead a project 聽上去還可以，但是 conduct a project 的話不太清楚，你的 project 是甚麼...

後來真的舉了好多例子，我才意識到 conduct 之後的那個名詞要是那種一聽上去就知道是甚麼的東西。比如說 conduct a research, conduct xx operation, conduct fire drill, conduct an experiment. 這些都是你一聽就知道實際上會是怎麼的操作。 但是 conduct a project，你可能會問那 project 是關於甚麼？ 在這裡 project 就不太適用。

結論就是 conduct/carry out 後面跟的名詞，應該是一件有明確指引的事，native speaker 聽上去才會覺得合理。

# Impression & cognition

文章裡有一個 activity 是要配對詞，我填了 impression - thinking ；cognition - sense; Lisa 說我錯了 Q_Q，sense 是更加像 impression，這裡不像是我以為的 "觸覺"" 視覺 "啊那種 sense，更是一種感覺，比如" 我感覺你好事近了 "，" 我感覺你是個很成功的人 "，就是這種感覺（感受上的）！

# 課後感受

下次我應該要多問在甚麼情況下用甚麼詞，然後還可以問某些詞語，我用起來會不會讓你覺得不舒服。今天我們聽完一個錄音，我回答問題的時候，我說了錄音裡提到的人 Refuse doing something that he can't do. 然後 Lisa 老師馬上糾正我說我這樣用 refuse，會讓人覺得太過倔強 / 固執了，但是本來錄音裡的並不帶這個用意。她說我可以用 he admits he can't do xxxxxx 用 admits 或者其他詞會更加的溫和點～
